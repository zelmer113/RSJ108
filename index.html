WikiHow: How to Write a Book Review

Activity Time: Depends on your Writing Pace!
Difficulty Rating: 4 Books Out of 5

Sourced from WikiHow, Co-authored by Christopher Taylor, PhD

After reading the book you would like to write a review on, these are the steps you can take to prepare your information. All text below is directly from the WikiHow article.

  • Required Items

  • A laptop or pen and paper
  • The book you would like to review
  • Access to the internet
  • Sticky notes (optional)
  • Highlighters (optional)

  1. Think about the book's genre and/or field of study.

    If necessary, use outside sources to familiarize yourself with the field of study and the genre of the book.

    Try summarizing the major sections of the book you’re reviewing to help understand how it’s structured.

  2. Determine the major arguments and themes of the book.

    The theme is often a lesson or overall message that the reader perceives between the lines.

  3. Consider the author's writing style.

    Remember that genre is a category of writing and style is the manner in which a subject is expressed or performed.

  4. Think about how well the author develops the major areas or points in the book.

    Locating gaps in the timeframe or character development in fiction, or the coverage and analysis in nonfiction can help you think critically.

  5. Make note of the book's format, if relevant.

    Elements like the book's layout, binding, typography, etc. can provide framing and context for the book.

  6. Consider any literary devices in the book.

    Take notes on the book's character, plot, setting, symbols, mood, or tone and how they relate to the overall theme of the book.

  7. Think about how unique the book is.

    The author may be trying to challenge or expand the existing rules and norms of a genre.

    Consider how the book does this, and how this may affect the intended audience's reception of the book.

  8. Assess how successful the book is.

    Was the author successful in carrying out the overall purposes of the book, and did you feel satisfied with the book's ending?

  9. Creating a first draft of the review.

    Once you have summarized the book and discussed the main themes and aspects, shift into your critical analysis.

    Examine the strengths and weaknesses of the book, and discuss whether you would recommend the book to others.

  10. Polishing the review.

    To gain perspective, put the review aside for a few days and then come back to it with fresh eyes.
